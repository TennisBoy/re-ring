# Re:Ring

A 2-minute pitch site for **SHAD 2026 — Design & Entrepreneurship** at TMU.

**How might we reimagine the life cycle of everyday goods to minimize waste and drive sustainable innovation?**

Re:Ring rethinks cheap metal jewelry. Instead of a solid copper/nickel ring that gets mined,
worn for a season, and buried to corrode forever, Re:Ring is a **3D-printed plant-based (PLA)
core electroplated with a micron-thin skin of real metal** — full shine, a fraction of the mined
metal — designed to be **taken back, re-plated, and composted**. A line becomes a loop.

## The pitch

`index.html` is a self-contained, full-screen click-through deck (~6 beats for 2:00).

- **Navigate:** arrow keys / scroll, or the dots on the right.
- **Rehearse:** the *Rehearse · 2:00* button (bottom-left) opens a timed speaker script.

Just open `index.html` in any browser — no build step, no dependencies.

## Stakeholders

- **Gen-Z** — custom, printed-to-order, priced like fast fashion.
- **Environmentalists** — far less mined metal per ring; a closed take-back loop.
- **Government** — a fundable pilot that fits Canada's Right-to-Repair / circular-economy direction.

## Note on claims

- PLA does **not** biodegrade in nature — it needs industrial composting. The deck reflects this.
- The "less mined metal" figure is a design-stage estimate, to be confirmed with real geometry.

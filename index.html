Re:Ring's life cycle

A ring, in the shape of a closed loop.

Every stage feeds the next.

Plant sugar becomes filament, filament becomes a ring, the ring is plated, worn, repaired — then returned, stripped, and composted. The metal re-plates the next batch. Nothing dead-ends.

✓ Up to ~90% less mined metal* · ✓ renewable core · ✓ built to return

2-minute pitch script

  1. Hook · 0:150:00 "We love our jewelry — but a cheap ring lasts a season and a landfill forever. What if it came back instead?"
  2. Problem · 0:200:15 Old rings are a straight line: mine the metal, wear it out, bury it. In the landfill copper and nickel corrode and leach for generations. It's a line, not a loop.
  3. Idea · 0:250:35 Re:Ring keeps the metal *look* and drops the metal *bulk*. We 3D-print the shape from plant-based PLA, then electroplate a few microns of real metal — full shine, tiny footprint.
  4. New loop · 0:251:00 And we design it to return. A take-back program strips the metal skin to re-plate the next batch and composts the core. Every stage feeds the next — the loop closes.
  5. Who wins · 0:201:25 Gen-Z gets custom, affordable drops. Environmentalists get up to ~90% less mined metal. Government gets a ready-made Right-to-Repair pilot.
  6. Ask · 0:151:45 Fund the take-back pilot. Let's turn a throwaway into a loop. That's Re:Ring — jewelry that comes back.
